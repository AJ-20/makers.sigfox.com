SIGFOX Will Announce Two Major Agreements and Present on Future of IoT

LABEGE, France – Oct. 27, 2015 – SIGFOX will present at three sessions during European Utility Week in Vienna, Nov. 3-5. The company will announce a partnership with a major French nationwide water company that is using the Internet of Things to improve service and efficiency, and an agreement with a global maker of low-cost smart electricity meters to manufacture meters tailored to the SIGFOX network. The SIGFOX team and their partners will be available for interviews at booth B. e17.

The vast majority of smart utilities that will be connected to the IoT will require very simple, economical and low-energy connectivity to transmit or receive their data. In addition to meeting those requirements, SIGFOX supports a guaranteed service-level agreement for customers, and simple subscription-based pricing. Those advantages, all packaged with one contract, will allow makers of chips, systems on chips, modules and end products, as well as platform and app developers to seamlessly scale their solutions wherever the SIGFOX network is operating.

SIGFOX Booth: B. e17: The company’s booth will demonstrate innovative utility solutions for gas, electricity and water providers.

About SIGFOX

SIGFOX is the world’s leading provider of dedicated cellular connectivity for Internet of Things and machine-to-machine communications. The company’s network complements existing high-bandwidth systems by providing economical, energy-efficient two-way transmission of small quantities of data over long distances, thus lowering barriers to wide implementation of IoT and M2M solutions, and greatly extending the battery and service life of connected devices. SIGFOX, currently deployed in nine countries, today covers 1 million square kilometers with a population of 165 million people. Five million devices already are registered in the network. Corporate headquarters are in Labège, France, and the company has offices in Paris, Boston, Dubai, Madrid and San Francisco.
